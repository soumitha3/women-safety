SAFETY TIPS

Stay alert and conscious of your surroundings. Avoid using headphones or being engrossed in your phone while walking in unfamiliar areas.

Trust your instincts. If something feels off or uncomfortable, take action accordingly. It's okay to say no and remove yourself from a situation that doesn't feel safe.

Inform someone you trust about your plans, especially if you're going out alone. Use location-sharing features on your phone with trusted friends or family.

Taking self-defense classes can boost your confidence and provide valuable skills. Consider carrying personal safety devices like pepper spray or a personal alarm if legal and permitted.

Stick to well-lit streets and populated areas, especially at night. Avoid shortcuts through dark alleys or isolated areas.

Keep your belongings secure and be mindful of your purse, phone, and other valuables. Avoid displaying expensive jewelry or items that might attract unwanted attention.

Travel with friends or in groups whenever possible. There is safety in numbers, and potential attackers may be deterred by a group.

Opt for reputable transportation services, and share your ride details with someone you trust. Check the driver's details and ensure they match the information in the app.

Keep an eye on your drink at all times to prevent tampering. Don't accept drinks from strangers unless you witness them being prepared.

Save emergency contacts in your phone and know how to quickly dial emergency services. Familiarize yourself with local emergency numbers and services.

Be cautious about sharing personal information online. Adjust privacy settings on social media and be mindful of location tagging.

Stay informed about safety resources and organizations in your community. Attend workshops or seminars on personal safety.
